Critical Information in nature
The community of natural science took centuries to appreciate the essence of information in dealing with a physical
system, be it a rudimentary particle or an early universe phenomenon; in fact everything in the middle. What we see
and what we cannot anticipate within our mortal limitations,falls under the category of natural events. Starting from
everyday facts to questionnaires on even inexistance of an object, all carry the inevitable validation with information.
Isn’t it intriguing that although we cannot hold in our grip the information, all we can ever do is to describe the
phenomena in terms of information only? The information we endow for a Newtonian particle is the mass of its centre
of gravity, and its corresponding velocity vector, likewise the information for a classical fluid is encapsulated within
its density profile and velocity field, which for a quantum particle would be its spectrum of eigen-possibilities. It’s not
ending there dear reader, for it extends from the microscopic quantum realm to the surface of a black hole; even the
natural structures around us will indeed surprise us quite a lot when looked at its functions, in terms of information.
In fact recent research on the physics behind mind and human cognition entails the rudiments of information in the
perceptions of each and every single instance of our life. John Wheeler, the famous physicist who brought back the
craze of general relativity back in 50’s after the second world war had an extremely appreciated article named ‘it
from bit’, where he writes -
Every it, every particle, every field of force, even the spacetime continuum itself - derives its function, its meaning,
its very existence entirely - even if in some contexts indirectly - from the apparatus-elicited answers to yes-or-no
questions, binary choices, bits
establishing the ubiquity of information in all physical realisations possible. One cannot fall short in exaggerating
how information engulfs the reality we belong to, but specifying ourselves in the effectiveness of information in
natural structures that we find on an everyday basis, I believe would be the first step in encouraging how critical this
idea is even in simplest innocuous things. The wonder to ponder here goes like this - the spontaneous emergence
of things that make up our surroundings were not driven by any external agent, rather billions of years of periodic
exposure of energy from sun, and resources of earth-surface reinforced the complexity and the adaptive nature of
nature. The word complexity has been very fascinating in recent times due to its simplicity within a huge interactive
environment, immersed within an indeterminable randomness, and the physics of information can only capture its
intricacies. But it is suitable now to arm ourselves with particular contexts where we can definitively mention how
information manifests.
The idea goes in a slightly abstract but in a very general direction. Every
hierarchical structures that pop(-ped)-up spontaneously in the nature are what
we will be discussing here, a tree, a lightning bolt, the sidewise branching of
river passage, the taxonomic tree of life (capturing diversities in genomic
sequences of life forms on earth), mammalian blood vessels, neuronal networks
the body, the power-hierarchy in the society (the monetary centralisation of
power in handful riches with working class of millions, without any money at all),
falls under the big hood of self-similar networks. But for that we need to have a
slight introduction of what correlation, and criticality is in any many-body
dynamics. Conceptually, correlation is the extent of how distant subparts are
statistically related among each other, i.e. the correlation is a statistical measure
of their likeness during the dynamics. Take the structure of a tree, just by your
window, don’t you imagine why any subpart is exactly like the whole tree itself, only scaled down by some constant
factor. The naked leafless skeleton of a tree becomes unreasonably surprising when asked, how come the branch
angles from toe to tip are exactly the same! Somehow the instance of a branching in the canopy is acquiring the
information how the tree branched all the way back near the trunk. Some information must have propagated in time
throughout the evolution of the system. It is the temporal correlation between the states of that system, which near
the critical point diverges out, i.e. the correlation length, which one can think of as the range of informational
influence, blows up to large values at the critical point. Criticality is another trait of complex systems, where the
subparts interact among each other, and give rise to many interesting emergent properties that weren’t present in
the constituents; as P.W. Anderson discussed in his article, More is Different. Such systems can have a situation
where they exhibit long-range correlation, spatially, temporally or functionally i.e. arbitrarily distant subparts are
highly correlated there.
Nature is all about the harmonious orchestration of information between its small divisions, that we can consider
the members of the ecosystem and even beyond. Life itself is a critical phenomena as has been recently pointed
out, where large molecules clumped via self-organisation to form more and more functionally sophisticated
structures. Tree, as we mentioned, carries a long-range correlation in its evolution, giving rise to fractal-like
structures that are literally ubiquitous in nature. Not only so, electric breakdown in solid insulators leave out the
trajectory that looks exactly self-similar to itself at all scales, like the tree. It is called the Lichtenberg figure, named
after German physicist George Christophe Lichtenberg who studied insulator materials and their electric discharge
patterns in them. Nowadays electrons from a particle accelerator are injected with a lot of momentum into acrylic
slabs, to the extent where those negatively charged particles are just minimally stable enough not to break loose by
discharging. One can imagine a huge rumbling and chaotic sea of electrons somehow shackled within the crystal
gaps within that solid insulator. But now when the slab is hit by a pointed portion, the chaotic electron sea finds a
preferred direction and all hell breaks loose. They flow out in the hierarchical branching pattern that is exactly similar

Where is the information in these scenarios? Being critical of the criticality in nature, one might argue Information is
Pattern. The essence of any aspect can only be unambiguously extrapolated when it consists of some unique
sequence that carries with itself the grammar to decode it. The information encoded within the structure of a tree, or
a lichtenberg figure, is the branched patterns of its sub-structures, that constitutes the whole. At the critical point,
the system replicates the pattern at all scales, and information propagates in that way. So the fact that nature is full
of fractals, is just the consequence of spontaneous criticality and long-range correlation, which sheds more
phenomenological light on the geometric origin of fractality, other than excessive popularisation of the word itself.
There has been extensive work on spontaneously forming networks in the society, starting from social media
connectivity, collaboration networks, administration networks, all have this crucial property of being similar to itself
at all the length and time scales; as if somehow information is propagating throughout the system by replicating its
minimal patterns. Our own existence is a consequence of this, otherwise we wouldn't feel surprised to realise it’s
only us and our living consciousness that are very unique in the entire solar system, or even the whole galaxy we
are within.
